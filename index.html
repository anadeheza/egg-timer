How to cook the perfect eggs

Soft Boiled (6-7 minutes)

Runny yolk: The yolk stays liquid and golden while the whites are just set

Perfect for: dipping, spreading and salads, the liquid yolk adds exquisite taste and texture

Medium Boiled (8-9 minutes)

Jammy yolk: The yolk is partially set but still soft and creamy while the whites are firm

Perfect for: honestly everything, it is the most versatile style

Hard Boiled (10 minutes)

Firm yolk: The yolk is solid with a paler yellow color and the whites are fully firm

Perfect for: deviled eggs, egg sandwiches, as a snack (easy to transport and eat without getting dirty)

Avoid

+10 minutes: The eggs are overcooked, with a rubbery, dry, grayer yolk

Eggs lose some nutrients when overcooked!

-4 minutes: The eggs are undercooked, this will upset your stomach and you could get salmonella (depending on where you bought the egg)

Remember: raw eggs have a lower digestibility (50/60%) compared to cooked eggs (90%) and protein syntesis is not as good when undercooked

Tips

Cool your eggs for 5 minutes right after boiling to stop the cooking (they'll be easier to peel too!)

Cooler eggs: If you keep your eggs on the fridge (reccommended but not in the door), you ought to add 1-2 minutes to the boiling, depending on how cold they where

Egg size: You may have to add or take 1-2 minutes from boiling depending on the size of your egg
